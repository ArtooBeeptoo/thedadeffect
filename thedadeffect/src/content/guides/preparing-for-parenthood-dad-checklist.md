### Get the Gear Ready
*   **Car Seat:** Research and install a car seat well before the due date. Practice putting it in and taking it out.
*   **Crib/Bassinet:** Assemble and set up the baby's sleeping space. Ensure it meets safety standards.
*   **Diapers and Wipes:** Stock up on various sizes. You'll go through a lot!
*   **Bottles and Formula (if applicable):** If you're not exclusively breastfeeding, have these on hand.
*   **Clothes:** Newborn and 0-3 month sizes. Don't overdo it, they grow fast.

### Prepare Your Home
*   **Baby-Proofing:** Start early. Outlet covers, cabinet locks, securing furniture.
*   **Cleaning:** Give the house a good deep clean before the baby arrives.
*   **Meal Prep:** Cook and freeze meals for the first few weeks. Future you will thank you.

### Mental & Emotional Prep
*   **Talk to Your Partner:** Discuss expectations, fears, and hopes.
*   **Read Books/Articles:** Educate yourself on newborn care, feeding, and sleep.
*   **Connect with Other Dads:** Share experiences and get advice.
*   **Self-Care Plan:** Figure out how you'll maintain your own well-being during this demanding time.

### Financial Readiness
*   **Budget Review:** Adjust your budget for new baby expenses.
*   **Insurance:** Understand your health insurance coverage for the baby.
*   **Paternity Leave:** Plan your time off from work.

This checklist is a starting point. Every journey is unique, but being prepared will make the transition smoother.
